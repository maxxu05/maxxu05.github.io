Curriculum Vitae (PDF)

I work at the intersection of applied and theoretical machine learning for time-series data, with a strong application focus on mobile health sensors. My goal is to develop temporal machine learning methodologies to be used with wearable health sensors.

As a Georgia Tech Machine Learning PhD student, I work with Jim Rehg as my advisor and have recieved the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and Georgia Tech Presidential Fellowship awards. I am also a part of the NIH mHealth Center.

My previous bioinformatics research was on understanding how the overexpression of the GABA genes affects cancer prognosis. I was advised by Soma Sengupta and Daniel Krummel at the University of Cincinnati.
